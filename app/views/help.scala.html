About Transfer Digital Records

Transfer Digital Records (TDR) is a secure digital transfer service from The National Archives (TNA) that helps you as a Public Record Body prepare and transfer your selected digital public records to TNA for permanent preservation and publication.

Under the 20-year rule, you must transfer selected and sensitivity-reviewed digital public records by 20 years after they are created at the latest. This is usually determined by your date last modified. Until then, you must safeguard the records and actively manage their digital continuity to maintain your ability to find, open, understand and trust them over time and through change. We are very happy to accept early transfers of digital records and encourage you to transfer digital records prior to the 20-year deadline. There are advantages to you transferring early; once records are transferred, you will no longer need to manage their digital continuity. We also advise taking a 'little and often' approach to transferring digital records to The National Archives.

TDR is an improvement on our existing service. We have created a product which provides a preparation area where records are uploaded in order to prepare for transfer to TNA. These uploaded records in TDR continue to be the responsibility of and owned by you, the Transferring Body. For example, while your records are in TDR, you will be responsible for handling any FOI requests. The records are only transferred to TNA during the final stage of the TDR process.

TDR is being built using an Agile approach. Private beta is an early Agile stage where the service is still being developed and does not yet offer the full range of features that we intend to support. For example, TDR can currently only accept records that are open (i.e. no FOI exemptions apply), crown copyright and in English.

At this private beta stage, the service is also only available to an invited group of users. Over time, we will make TDR available to more public record bodies and will continue to develop the service in consultation with our users, to add support for closed records and more complex types of records.

Secured access

In order to keep the service and your account secure from attack, TDR uses Multi-factor Authentication (MFA), which needs two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate and access the service. This means you will be required to sign in each time using a unique generated 'one-time password' (OTP) code that confirms who you are. This can be set up with a free-to-download authenticator app on your smartphone, such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP. Other authenticators are available.

Free-to-download apps are available from the App Store for iPhone, or 'Google Play' and 'Play Store' for Android.

File formats

To ensure that your records have the best possible chance of being successfully uploaded and passing TDR validation checks, we ask that you read the following information about the file formats we accept before proceeding to upload your records:

  • We cannot accept files that are password protected or encrypted
  • During private beta, we cannot accept zip files
  • Files such as thumbnail images (thumbs.db) and system files are not usually selected for permanent preservation. We will accept these but strongly advise you remove them prior to upload.

To identify these files, search your folder for the file extensions (thumb.dbs, .exe and .zip). For password protected files, click to view folder options and select ‘hidden files and folders’. These files should usually be identified as part of your selection and sensitivity review process.

We recommend that you check your files prior to upload to ensure that you upload only selected records intended for preservation. During the upload and checking process, if we detect files we cannot accept, you will be notified on screen and you will be unable to proceed with your transfer.

Glossary

Each of the following terms is defined within the context of Transfer Digital Records (TDR).

Anti-virus scan

A check performed on digital files to detect the presence of computer viruses or other malware. We do not accept files that contain viruses or malware and so if these are detected the upload will fail.

Consignment

A consignment is a group of selected records being prepared and transferred to The National Archives. Sometimes referred to as a batch.

Consignment reference

Each consignment of digital records uploaded to TDR for transfer to The National Archives is assigned a consignment reference which can be identified as a series of letters and numbers. Please quote this reference if you need to contact us about your records transfer.

Creating body

The government department or body that originally created the records being transferred. This is often the same as the body that transfers the records.

Crown Copyright

Under section 163 of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, Crown Copyright is defined as works made by officers or servants of the Crown in the course of their duties. This includes material created by ministers, civil servants and government departments and agencies. Copyright can also be assigned or transferred to the Crown by the legal owner of the copyright. Most, though not all, Public Records are Crown Copyright.

Data integrity check

When you upload your digital records to TDR, we carry out data integrity checks to help ensure that files have not been corrupted during the upload process. The integrity check uses checksums which are like digital fingerprints used to verify that no changes have been made to the data.

The checksum hash we use is SHA 256.

Departmental Records Officer

A Departmental Records Officer (DRO) is a person appointed by a government department or agency as being responsible for the management of the records of that organisation. The DRO must authorise any transfer of records to The National Archives and is responsible for ensuring that the records are properly sensitivity reviewed prior to transfer.

Freedom of Information (FOI) exemption

FOI exemptions exist to protect information that should not be disclosed. A list of exemptions can be found in Part II of the Freedom of Information Act, at sections 21 to 44. Records transferred to The National Archives will be released to the public (‘opened’) unless you apply one or more FOI exemptions.

Metadata

Data that provides additional information about digital records. For example, information that could help a user find or understand a record, locate other related information or understand how the record was originally created or used.

Private beta

The Transfer Digital Records service is still being developed and is currently at a 'private beta' stage. At this stage in its development, TDR does not yet offer the full range of features which our users require (for example, TDR can currently only accept records which are Open, Crown Copyright and in English). The service is currently only available to an invited group of users.

Public Records

Records of, or held in, any department of Her Majesty’s Government in the United Kingdom or records of any office, commission or other body or establishment whatsoever under Her Majesty’s Government in the United Kingdom, as defined in paragraph 2 of the First Schedule to the Public Records Act 1958.

Most records held by the UK central government and related bodies are Public Records.

Records held by local authorities, educational institutions, businesses or private individuals are not usually Public Records. If you need advice on whether the records you hold are Public Records please contact us.

Records selection

The process of evaluating your records to determine which should be transferred to The National Archives for permanent preservation, in line with the Public Records Act. Selection must be completed prior to using TDR.

Sensitivity review

Before you transfer records to The National Archives, you must review them to identify any sensitive information and apply the relevant FOI exemption(s). Transferred records will be released to the public except where FOI exemptions have been applied.

Series reference

A reference assigned to a set of archived records. Series references generally indicate the creating department and the subject matter of the records.

Records transfer

The process of formally transferring custody of public records that have been selected for permanent preservation to The National Archives (or other approved Place of Deposit for public records).

Transferring body

The government department or body that is transferring records for permanent preservation. This is often the same as the body that originally created the records.
