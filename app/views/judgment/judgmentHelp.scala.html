About this service

Transfer Digital Records (TDR) is The National Archives’ secure digital transfer service that enables Public Record Bodies to upload, prepare and then export selected digital public records to transfer them to The National Archives (TNA) for permanent archiving and publication.

For anyone involved in recording judgments, this service is used to send the final approved judgment to TNA for preservation and publication. This is a two stage process:

  • First you will upload your judgment into TDR
  • Then you will be prompted to export your judgment for transfer to TNA

Before you begin

Web browsers

We do not support Internet Explorer (IE11). We advise you to use a different browser such as Edge, Chrome, Safari or Firefox to ensure you can use this service and all of its features.

Secured access

In order to keep the service and your account secure from attack, TDR uses Multi-factor Authentication (MFA), which needs two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate and access the service. This means you will be required to sign in each time using a unique generated ‘one-time password’ (OTP) code that confirms who you are. This can be set up with a free-to-download authenticator app on your smartphone, such as Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator, or FreeOTP. Other authenticators are available.

During upload and checking, if we detect a file we cannot accept, you will be notified on screen.

File format

Double-check your judgment prior to upload to ensure you are sending us the correct file.

Only upload a single Microsoft Word (.docx) document per transfer, using the template supplied by the Judiciary Office Judgments Helpdesk.

During upload and checking, if we detect a file we cannot accept, you will be notified on screen.

Supplementary material

Occasionally other supporting documents, such as photos, need preserving with the judgment. When this happens, they will need to be emailed to the Case Law editorial team with the transfer reference of the judgment they relate to. Full details on how to do this can be found here.

Contact us

If you have a query regarding the transfer process, the status of your transfer or more general queries regarding the suitability of records for transfer, file format or transfer agreement criteria, email the Judiciary Office Judgments Helpdesk at Judgments@@judiciary.uk.

Transfer Digital Records Glossary

Each of the following terms is defined within the context of Transfer Digital Records (TDR).

Anonymisation Order

Specific conditions formerly assigned to protect the identity of one or more parties.

Authenticator app

A software app that can be downloaded and configured to produce a one-time password (OTP) code unique to the user of the device.

Browser

An application used to access the internet, such as Edge, Chrome, Safari or Firefox.

Judgment

A record of a court judgment or tribunal decision.

Microsoft Word (.docx) document

A specific electronic file format used for creating documents.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

A security feature that uses two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate a genuine account holder. In TDR this is the user’s unique password in combination with an OTP code generated in a configured authenticator app.

Neutral citation

A reference that makes it easier to find judgments in online resources, such as BAILII. The abbreviation indicates the court in which the case was heard, and the number indicates the case number.

One-time password (OTP)

A unique code specific to a configured account that refreshes every 30 to 60 seconds.

Replacement versions

An amended replacement version of an existing judgment that has already been published by TNA.

Supplementary material

Additional specified documents that are directly associated with an individual judgment.

Transfer Digital Records (TDR)

A product that allows specific documents to be transferred electronically and securely to TNA via a web browser.

Transfer reference

A unique code assigned to your individual transfer (e.g. TDR-2022-1234) that allows us to identify the transaction and judgment within it.

Upload

The action of copying a file into TDR, or the copied file itself.

Validation checks

Digital file checking that includes anti-virus check, file authentication, and file-format identification.
