About this service

Transfer Digital Records (TDR) is a secure digital transfer service from The National Archives (TNA) that enables Public Record Bodies to upload, prepare and then transfer selected digital public records to TNA for permanent archiving and publication.

For anyone involved in recording judgments, this service is used to send the final approved judgment and press summaries to TNA for preservation and publication on the 'Find Case Law' website https://caselaw.nationalarchives.gov.uk.

Sending judgments via TDR is in two stages:

  • First you will upload your judgment onto TDR
  • Then your document will be scanned to check for viruses etc.
  • If your document passes these checks, it will automatically be submitted for transfer to TNA

Before you begin

Web browsers

We do not support Internet Explorer. We advise you to use a different browser such as Edge, Chrome or Firefox to ensure you can use this service and all of its features.

Secured access

In order to keep the service and your account secure, TDR uses Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), which needs two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate and grant access to the service. This means you are required to sign in each time using a unique generated 'one-time password' (OTP) code that confirms who you are. This can be set up with a free-to-download authenticator app on your smartphone, such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP. Other authenticators are also available.

Free-to-download apps are available from the App Store for iPhone, or 'Google Play' and 'Play Store' for Android.

File format

You must only upload a single Microsoft Word (.docx) document per transfer, using the template supplied by the Judiciary Office Judgments Helpdesk. During upload and checking, if we detect a file we cannot accept, you will be notified on screen. For more information about the types of files we accept, contact @Messages("judiciary.email")

Supplementary material

If supporting documents, such as photos, need to be preserved with a judgment, they must be emailed to the TNA Find Case Law editorial team with the transfer reference of the judgment they relate to. Full details on how to do this can be found 'Supplementary material'.

Contact us

If you have a query regarding asking for a TDR account, the transfer process, the status of your transfer, or more general queries regarding the suitability of records for transfer, file format or transfer agreement criteria, email the Judiciary Office Judgments Helpdesk at @Messages("judiciary.email").

Set up two-step authentication

What is two-step authentication and why do I need to use an OTP?

An OTP (one-time password) is a unique code that confirms your identity. It’s generated by an authenticator app on your smartphone. You can download one of these free authenticator apps: Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP. These apps are available on the App Store for iPhone, or Google Play for Android.

To keep the service and your account secure, you must sign in to your TDR account using an OTP in addition to your personal password.

Step 1: Install an authenticator app

Before you continue, make sure you have an authenticator app installed on your smartphone. You can only use Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP.

Step 2: Set up your authenticator app

Open the authenticator app on your smartphone.

Create a new account in the app by scanning the QR code displayed on your computer screen.

For Google Authenticator: Choose "Scan a QR code".

For Microsoft Authenticator: Tap the '+' icon to add a new account. If you can’t see this, tap the three dots in the top right corner. Then select "Other (Google, Facebook, etc.)" to open the QR scanner.

For FreeOTP: Tap the QR code icon at the top of your screen.

Once you’ve scanned the QR code, the app will create a unique account to use with TDR and display a 6-digit code that refreshes regularly.

Step 3: Enter the code

Enter the 6-digit code from your app into the "One-time code" box on your computer without spaces. Make sure you enter the code before it refreshes (every 30 to 60 seconds).

Click "Submit" before the code changes in the app.

Transfer Digital Records Glossary

Each of the following terms is defined within the context of Transfer Digital Records (TDR).

Anonymisation Order

Specific conditions formerly assigned to protect the identity of one or more parties.

Authenticator app

A software app that can be downloaded onto a smartphone and configured to produce a one-time password (OTP) code unique to the user of the device.

Browser

An application used to access the internet, such as Edge, Chrome or Firefox.

Judgment

A record of a court judgment or tribunal decision.

Microsoft Word (.docx) document

A specific electronic file format used for creating documents.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

A security feature that uses two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate a genuine account holder. In TDR this includes the user's unique password and an OTP code generated in a configured authenticator app.

Neutral citation

A reference that makes it easier to find judgments in online resources, such as BAILII. The abbreviation indicates the court in which the case was heard, and the number indicates the case number.

One-time password (OTP)

A unique code generated in an authenticator app that is specific to the user of a configured account.

Update or revision

An amended replacement version of an existing judgment that has already been uploaded to TNA for publishing.

Supplementary material

Additional specified documents that are directly associated with an individual judgment.

Transfer Digital Records (TDR)

A product that allows specific documents to be transferred electronically and securely to TNA via a web browser.

Transfer reference

A unique reference code assigned to your individual transfer (e.g. TDR-2022-1234) that allows us to identify the transaction and judgment within it.

Upload

The action of copying a file into TDR, or the copied file itself.

Validation checks

Digital file checking that includes anti-virus check, file authentication, and file-format identification.
