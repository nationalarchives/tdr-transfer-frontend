About this service

Transfer Digital Records (TDR) is a secure digital transfer service from The National Archives (TNA) that enables Public Record Bodies to upload, prepare and then transfer selected digital public records to TNA for permanent archiving and publication.

For anyone involved in recording judgments, this service is used to send the final approved judgment to TNA for preservation and publication on the 'Find Case Law' website https://caselaw.nationalarchives.gov.uk.

Sending via TDR is a two stage process:

  • First you will upload your judgment into TDR
  • Then you will be prompted to export your judgment for transfer to TNA

Before you begin

Web browsers

We do not support Internet Explorer (IE11). We advise you to use a different browser such as Edge, Chrome, Safari or Firefox to ensure you can use this service and all of its features.

Secured access

In order to keep the service and your account secure from attack, TDR uses Multi-factor Authentication (MFA), which needs two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate and access the service. This means you will be required to sign in each time using a unique generated 'one-time password' (OTP) code that confirms who you are. This can be set up with a free-to-download authenticator app on your smartphone, such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP. Other authenticators are available.

Free-to-download apps are available from the App Store for iPhone, or 'Google Play' and 'Play Store' for Android.

File format

Double-check your judgment prior to upload to ensure you are sending us the correct file.

Only upload a single Microsoft Word (.docx) document per transfer, using the template supplied by the Judiciary Office Judgments Helpdesk, contactable at @Messages("judiciary.email")

During upload and checking, if we detect a file we cannot accept, you will be notified on screen.

Supplementary material

Occasionally other supporting documents, such as photos, need preserving with the judgment. When this happens, they will need to be emailed to TNA Case Law editorial team with the transfer reference of the judgment they relate to. Full details on how to do this can be found here.

Contact us

If you have a query regarding asking for a TDR account, the transfer process, the status of your transfer, or more general queries regarding the suitability of records for transfer, file format or transfer agreement criteria, email the Judiciary Office Judgments Helpdesk at @Messages("judiciary.email").

What is OTP and why do I need to use it?

In order to keep the service and your account secure from attack, TDR requires Multi-factor Authentication (MFA), which uses two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate access to the service. This means that, in addition to your personal password, you will be required to sign in each time using a 'One-time password' (OTP) code from an authenticator app, which you set up to generate a unique single use code that confirms who you are. Free authenticator apps can be downloaded onto your smartphone, such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP. Other authenticators are available.

Free-to-download apps are available from the App Store for iPhone, or 'Google Play' and 'Play Store'.

Part 2: Two-step authentication

Clicking the "Configure OTP, Update Password" link will take you to the "Sign in to your account" page:

  1. Ensure you have installed an authenticator app onto your smartphone before proceeding.
  2. To set up your app for use with TDR:
    1. Open your downloaded authenticator app on your mobile.
    2. Create a new TDR-related account on the app using the QR code displayed on your screen:
      • For Google Authenticator, click on the first option 'Scan a QR code'.
      • For Microsoft Authenticator, first click on the '+' icon to add an account. Note that you might need to click on the three little dots in the top right corner to have this option displayed. Then select "Other (Google, Facebook, etc.)" to access the QR scanner.
      • For Free OTP, click on the QR code icon located in the top banner of your smartphone screen.
    3. Scan the QR code displayed on your computer screen
    4. Your smartphone app will automatically create a unique OTP account to use with TDR sign in.
    5. You should now be able to see a 6-digit code that will refresh at regular intervals.
  3. In the "One-time code" box, type in the 6 digits without a space, ensuring that you have enough time to complete this page before the code refreshes (usually every 30 to 60 seconds).
  4. Click "Submit" before the code used expires in the app.

  1. Sign in by entering your "Email address" and "Password", then click "Sign in" to be taken to the "One-time code" section on this page:
  2. Enter a new 6-digit one-time code from the authenticator app that you set up when you registered. Enter the code without any spaces and click "Continue" before it refreshes.

Step 1: Check your file before uploading

You will be prompted to check that your document contains the following information:

  • Neutral citation
  • Name(s) of judge(s)
  • Name(s) of parties
  • Court
  • Judgment date

You must ensure your judgment includes all important information above before you start your upload.

Supplementary material

If you need to include additional documents with your judgment, you currently cannot upload them with your judgment as part of the transfer. Instead, click on the @Messages("nationalArchives.judgmentsEmail") link located in the middle of the screen to generate an automatic email with the transfer reference as the subject. Attach your supplementary documents and add any other relevant information before sending.

If your email application, for example Outlook, is not set up to automatically create the email for you, create your own email to send to the same address, manually adding the transfer reference as the subject.

Replacement versions

If your judgment is an amended replacement version for an existing judgment, use the same email method to notify the TNA Case Law editorial team. Include the neutral citation reference of the original judgment that it is replacing.

Anonymisation Orders

If your judgment has an anonymisation order, use the same email method to notify the TNA Case Law editorial team, include the neutral citation reference and any important information so that the editorial team can give your record the attention it needs. This procedure will become automated in time.

Press "Continue" when you are ready to move on.

Step 2: Upload the judgment

To upload your judgment, you can either:

  1. "Choose file" by browsing for a file stored on your computer, or
  2. "Drag and drop a single file" into the upload box.

    3. The file must be uploaded as a Microsoft Word document (.docx) and you must only upload a single file.

    Only valid Word files will be visible when using 'Choose file' to browse. If you attempt to 'Drag and drop' a different file format, you will see this error message and must choose a different valid Word file.

    When you have selected a valid file, the page will show the name of the file selected in green.

    If you selected a file in error, use the blue "Remove" link, to the right of the file name, to remove the file and start the selection again.

  3. Click "Start upload" to upload your judgment into TDR. You will see a progress bar as the file is uploaded.

    4. You will automatically be transferred to Step 3 on completion of your upload into TDR.

Step 3: Upload undergoes checks

Once uploaded, your judgment is automatically checked to ensure it is suitable for transfer. This will take several minutes so please keep your tab open if you navigate away to do something else whilst waiting.

If your upload passes the checks, you will be notified on screen with a "Success" message.

  1. You must click "Export" to transfer your successful upload from TDR to TNA and complete the process.

Error message

In the unlikely event that any issues are found, you will be notified on screen that your upload has failed and asked to begin your transfer again from the TDR Homepage.

Before you try again, you should first check the following:

  • Your internet connection is stable
  • Your file is a Microsoft Word document (.docx)
  • Your file is not corrupted or password protected by checking you can open it on your own computer

If you have confirmed yes to all then try again. If your file still fails, contact @Messages("nationalArchives.email") with details of the judgment you have tried to upload.

Step 4: Transfer complete

After clicking "Export", your upload will be transferred to TNA and, once received, you will automatically be taken to the "Transfer complete" page.

This will include a transfer reference (e.g. TDR-2022-1234). Make a note of this reference in case you need to contact us regarding your transfer or if you need to submit supporting documents, inform us about a replacement version, or inform us with detail of an anonymisation order.

Do not delete your original file yet!

Your file has only arrived at TNA and you will be notified again by email once the judgment has been received, processed by the Case Law editorial team, and published for viewing on the Find Case Law website.

Click "Return to start" to begin a new transfer, or "Sign Out" in the top right corner.

Transfer Digital Records Glossary

Each of the following terms is defined within the context of Transfer Digital Records (TDR).

Anonymisation Order

Specific conditions formerly assigned to protect the identity of one or more parties.

Authenticator app

A software app that can be downloaded onto a smartphone and configured to produce a one-time password (OTP) code unique to the user of the device.

Browser

An application used to access the internet, such as Edge, Chrome, Safari or Firefox.

Judgment

A record of a court judgment or tribunal decision.

Microsoft Word (.docx) document

A specific electronic file format used for creating documents.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

A security feature that uses two separate pieces of information in order to authenticate a genuine account holder. In TDR this is the user's unique password in combination with an OTP code generated in a configured authenticator app.

Neutral citation

A reference that makes it easier to find judgments in online resources, such as BAILII. The abbreviation indicates the court in which the case was heard, and the number indicates the case number.

One-time password (OTP)

A unique code specific to the user of a configured account that refreshes every 30 to 60 seconds.

Replacement versions

An amended replacement version of an existing judgment that has already been published by TNA.

Supplementary material

Additional specified documents that are directly associated with an individual judgment.

Transfer Digital Records (TDR)

A product that allows specific documents to be transferred electronically and securely to TNA via a web browser.

Transfer reference

A unique reference code assigned to your individual transfer (e.g. TDR-2022-1234) that allows us to identify the transaction and judgment within it.

Upload

The action of copying a file into TDR, or the copied file itself.

Validation checks

Digital file checking that includes anti-virus check, file authentication, and file-format identification.
